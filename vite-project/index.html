1960

In the 1960s, the woodblocks were systematically cataloged, with their printed images compiled into five volumes previously accessible only in the museum's reading room. However, earlier treatments with toxic anti-woodworm products posed preservation challenges, limiting physical access.

Now

Today, the digitized collection ensures these historical treasures can be admired without handling risks, preserving their legacy for future generations. These blocks, the foundation of 16th-century publishing, continue to inspire globally. Now digitized, they are celebrated through webinars, events, and initiatives like #WoodblockWednesday. Visitors can delve into their intricate beauty and historical significance through the Plantin-Moretus Museum's exhibitions and online database.
