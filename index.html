A truly monumental masterpiece, commissioned by King Philip II himself.

Only 1,200 copies were made, each intended for influential figures like the Pope and the King of Spain. In the 16th-century Netherlands, books like this were not just tools of knowledge, they were instruments of power and influence.

The Biblia Polyglotta also symbolized the Catholic Church's intellectual and cultural authority during the Counter-Reformation, reinforcing its dominance through scholarly and artistic excellence.

How many woodblocks do you think they have in the museum?

We have over 14000 blocks featuring botanical drawings, scientific diagrams, decorative initials, and intricate religious scenes.

This extraordinary collection, preserved at the Plantin-Moretus Museum, showcases a significant contribution to 16th-century publishing and art, including works by renowned botanists like Dodoens, Clusius, and Lobelius.

In the 1960s, the woodblocks were systematically cataloged, with their printed images compiled into five volumes previously accessible only in the museum's reading room. However, earlier treatments with toxic anti-woodworm products posed preservation challenges, limiting physical access.

1960

Now

Today, the digitized collection ensures these historical treasures can be admired without handling risks, preserving their legacy for future generations. These blocks, the foundation of 16th-century publishing, continue to inspire globally. Now digitized, they are celebrated through webinars, events, and initiatives like #WoodblockWednesday. Visitors can delve into their intricate beauty and historical significance through the Plantin-Moretus Museum's exhibitions and online database.
